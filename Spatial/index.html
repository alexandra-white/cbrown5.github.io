Spatial analysis in R

The R programming can work as a fully functioning GIS. It has several powerful advantages over many other GIS programs: you can program scripts for complete automation, it is relatively fast, and it is free.

An image of the velocity of climate change over the last several decades, made using R


The downside is that it is not easy to get started, particularly if you are not simultaneously a geo-statistical expert and a gun programmer. I am trying to make the jump easier for ecologists by providing courses and helpful blog posts here.


At the moment I have one course on spatial analysis in R, and more courses for R basics here.
